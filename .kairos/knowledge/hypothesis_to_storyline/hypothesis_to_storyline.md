# Hypothesis to Storyline

## Purpose
Transform research hypotheses and results into a compelling, logical narrative for publication.

## Narrative Arc Structure
1. **Context** — What is known? What gap exists?
2. **Question** — What specific question does this study address?
3. **Approach** — How was the question investigated?
4. **Key findings** — What are the main results? (order by importance)
5. **Interpretation** — What do the findings mean in context?
6. **Implications** — What changes because of this work?

## Common Pitfalls
- Starting with methods instead of the question
- Burying the key finding in supplementary results
- Claiming more than the data supports
- Mixing observation with interpretation
- Losing the thread between introduction and discussion

## Storyline Checklist
- [ ] One clear central question
- [ ] Each figure/table advances the narrative
- [ ] Results presented in logical order (not chronological)
- [ ] Discussion mirrors introduction structure
- [ ] Limitations acknowledged before implications
- [ ] Final paragraph: broader significance, not speculation
