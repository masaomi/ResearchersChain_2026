# Cover Letter Drafter (Journal Fit)

## Purpose
Draft concise, compelling cover letters that demonstrate fit with the target journal.

## Structure (3-4 paragraphs)

1. **Opening**: Manuscript title, type, and request for consideration
2. **Significance**: Why this work matters (1-2 key findings, broader impact)
3. **Journal fit**: Why THIS journal specifically (scope match, audience, recent related publications)
4. **Closing**: Declarations (no conflicts, not submitted elsewhere, all authors agree)

## Journal Fit Signals
- Reference 1-2 recent papers from the target journal that relate to your work
- Use the journal's language/terminology
- Match the scope statement on the journal website
- Highlight the aspect most relevant to the journal's audience

## Do NOT Include
- Detailed methods or full results
- Excessive self-citation
- Generic statements that apply to any journal
- Requests for specific reviewers (unless journal requires it)

## Length
- Typically 1 page, 3-4 paragraphs
- Editor reads dozens per day — be concise
