# Structured Abstract Composer

## Purpose
Generate well-structured abstracts that convey the study's essence within word limits.

## Standard Structure (IMRaD)

| Section | Content | Typical Length |
|---------|---------|---------------|
| **Background** | Gap in knowledge, why this matters | 1-2 sentences |
| **Objective** | Specific aim or hypothesis | 1 sentence |
| **Methods** | Design, subjects, key techniques | 2-3 sentences |
| **Results** | Key quantitative findings | 2-4 sentences |
| **Conclusions** | Interpretation and significance | 1-2 sentences |

## Writing Principles
- Lead with the most important finding
- Include specific numbers (effect sizes, p-values, sample sizes)
- Avoid abbreviations unless universally known
- No citations in the abstract
- Match the tone of the target journal
- Stay within word limit (typically 150-300 words)

## Self-Check
- [ ] Can a reader understand the study without reading the full paper?
- [ ] Are the key numbers present?
- [ ] Does the conclusion follow from the stated results?
- [ ] Is the clinical/scientific significance clear?
