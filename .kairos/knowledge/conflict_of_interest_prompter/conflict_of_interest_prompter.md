# Conflict of Interest Prompter

## Purpose
Systematically identify and disclose potential conflicts of interest before publication.

## COI Categories

| Category | Examples |
|----------|---------|
| **Financial** | Consulting fees, stock ownership, paid advisory roles, patent royalties |
| **Institutional** | Employer interest in outcome, departmental pressure |
| **Intellectual** | Strong prior commitment to a hypothesis, competing theory |
| **Personal** | Relationship with editors/reviewers, collaboration with competitors |
| **Funding** | Industry-sponsored research, funder influence on design/reporting |

## Disclosure Checklist (per author)
- [ ] Have I received funding from entities with interest in this outcome?
- [ ] Do I hold patents or IP related to this work?
- [ ] Am I a consultant or advisor for relevant companies?
- [ ] Do I hold equity in companies that could benefit?
- [ ] Could my institutional affiliations create perceived bias?
- [ ] Have I publicly committed to a position this paper supports?

## Disclosure Statement Template
"[Author] reports [grants/consulting fees/stock] from [entity], [relevant to / outside] the submitted work. [Author] declares no competing interests."

## Key Principle
Disclosure is not an accusation of bias — it is transparency that allows readers to evaluate the work in full context. When in doubt, disclose.
