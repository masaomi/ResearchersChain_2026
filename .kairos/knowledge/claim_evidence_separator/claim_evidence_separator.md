# Claim-Evidence Separator

## Purpose
Enforce clear separation between what was observed and what is being claimed.

## Three-Layer Framework

| Layer | Definition | Example |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| **Observation** | Raw data, direct measurement | "Gene X expression was 2.5-fold higher in treated vs control (padj=0.001)" |
| **Interpretation** | Inference from observations | "Gene X is upregulated by the treatment" |
| **Claim** | Broader conclusion or generalization | "Gene X plays a key role in the treatment response pathway" |

## Red Flags
- "Our data proves..." → Data supports, does not prove
- "Clearly, X causes Y" → Correlation observed; causation requires more evidence
- "This demonstrates that..." → Specify what was actually measured
- Unstated assumptions bridging observation to claim

## Writing Template
```
We observed [observation with numbers].
This suggests [interpretation], consistent with [prior evidence].
However, [limitation/alternative explanation] cannot be ruled out.
Further [experiment/analysis] would be needed to confirm [claim].
```

## Self-Check
- [ ] Can I point to the specific data supporting each claim?
- [ ] Have I distinguished correlation from causation?
- [ ] Are alternative interpretations acknowledged?
- [ ] Is the strength of language proportional to the evidence?
